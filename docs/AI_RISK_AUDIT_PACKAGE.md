## 1. Service One-Pager (What Buyers See)

### AI Vendor Risk Audit
**For law firms, accounting firms, and regulated professional services.**

**The question this answers:**
*"What specifically happens to our firm's AI capabilities, data, and disclosure obligations if our current AI vendors restructure, get acquired, change models, or change terms — and what's the architectural fix?"*

### What's Included
- **Vendor inventory.** Every AI tool, model, or API your firm currently uses — including shadow IT (the ones partners signed up for without telling IT).
- **Restructuring exposure analysis.** Each vendor's parent-company status, funding round positioning, and known acquisition/governance risks.
- **Contract clause review.** What your current AI vendor contracts actually allow them to change unilaterally — model versions, data retention, pricing, terms.
- **Data flow map.** Where privileged information physically goes when your attorneys use each tool. Which jurisdictions, which third parties, which retention windows.
- **Disclosure scenario modeling.** What you'd have to disclose to clients, opposing counsel, or the bench under three plausible vendor-restructuring scenarios.
- **Architectural recommendations.** Three specific recommendations, ranked by cost and disruption — including sovereign deployment as the structural fix.
- **Written deliverable.** A 10-12 page PDF report your General Counsel and Managing Partner can act on.
- **Presentation call.** 15-minute focused walkthrough with your decision-makers.

### What's NOT Included
- Software installation (this is diagnostic, not deployment)
- Ongoing advisory (one-time engagement)
- Vendor contract negotiation (we identify the risk; your GC negotiates the fix)

### Engagement Structure
- **Duration:** 2-3 weeks from kickoff
- **Time commitment from your team:** 2-3 hours total (kickoff call + 2-3 short data-gathering calls)
- **Deliverable:** Written report + 15-min presentation
- **Payment:** 50% on signed engagement letter, 50% on report delivery

### Pricing
- **$750** — firms under 100 attorneys
- **$1,500** — firms 100-500 attorneys
- **$3,000** — firms 500+ attorneys

### Credit Toward Pilot
**100% of the audit fee credits against an AION OS pilot deposit if your firm decides to deploy sovereign AI within 6 months of the audit delivery.**

## 5. Audit Report Template (Deliverable Skeleton)

**Format:** 10-12 page PDF. Branded header. Confidential watermark. Delivered as PDF + presentation deck.

### Section 1 — Executive Summary (1 page)
- Single-paragraph statement of overall exposure (Low / Moderate / High / Critical)
- Top 3 findings ranked by disclosure impact
- Top 3 recommendations ranked by cost and disruption

### Section 2 — Vendor Inventory (2 pages)
- Table of all AI tools/services in use across the firm
- Columns: Vendor name, product, business function, departments using it, attorneys using it, data types passed to it, contract owner, contract expiration, monthly spend
- Include shadow IT — the tools partners signed up for personally
- Findings: Unsanctioned tools, expired contracts, missing data processing addendums

### Section 3 — Restructuring Exposure Analysis (2 pages)
For each major vendor (top 3-5):
- Parent company status (private, public, subsidiary, JV)
- Recent funding/governance events (last 24 months)
- Known acquisition or restructuring rumors (cite sources)
- Concentration risk (what % of firm's AI workflow depends on this single vendor)
- Switching cost estimate

### Section 4 — Contract Clause Review (2 pages)
For each major vendor:
- What can the vendor change unilaterally? (model version, data retention, pricing, terms)
- Required notice period for material changes (usually 30 days)
- Termination rights (theirs and yours)
- Data return / deletion provisions on termination
- Liability caps
- Indemnification posture
- **Findings:** Clauses missing that *should* exist for a regulated firm

### Section 5 — Data Flow Map (1-2 pages)
- For each AI tool: where does the data physically go?
  - Geographic jurisdiction(s)
  - Sub-processors
  - Retention windows
  - Training-on-customer-data status
- Visual diagram if useful
- **Findings:** Privileged information leaving controllable jurisdictions

### Section 6 — Disclosure Scenario Modeling (1-2 pages)
Three plausible scenarios with estimated impact:

1. **Vendor Restructuring Scenario** — your primary AI vendor gets acquired, model changes mid-matter
   - What you'd disclose to clients
   - What you'd disclose to opposing counsel under e-discovery
   - What you'd disclose to the bench if asked

2. **Vendor Breach Scenario** — your vendor's data is exposed
   - Notification obligations under state privacy laws (GA, NY, CA)
   - Notification obligations under bar ethics rules
   - Reputational exposure

3. **Vendor Shutdown Scenario** — your vendor exits the market or pivots away from your use case
   - Continuity exposure
   - Data portability cost
   - Re-training cost on new vendor

### Section 7 — Architectural Recommendations (1-2 pages)
Three ranked recommendations:

1. **Tactical (low cost, low disruption):** Contract amendments, vendor diversification within the cloud category
2. **Strategic (moderate cost, moderate disruption):** Internal AI governance committee, vendor SLA tightening, redundant vendor coverage for critical workflows
3. **Architectural (higher cost, higher leverage):** Sovereign deployment for privileged-matter workflows — soft AION OS lead-in here

Each recommendation:
- Estimated cost range
- Estimated implementation timeline
- Estimated disruption to current workflows
- Risk reduction quantification

### Section 8 — Appendices
- Vendor reference links and source materials
- Glossary (sovereign AI, data residency, model versioning, etc.)
- About AION OS (1 paragraph)

## 6. Pilot Conversion Language

### When to introduce the pilot conversation
**Not in the audit itself.** The audit recommends sovereign deployment as one of three architectural options. The pilot conversation happens in the **presentation call** at the end.

### Presentation call structure (15 minutes)
- 0-3 min: Executive summary + overall risk rating
- 3-8 min: Top 3 findings (the ones with disclosure impact)
- 8-12 min: Three architectural recommendations
- 12-15 min: Pilot transition + next steps

**Why 15 minutes:** Decision-makers protect their calendars. A tight 15-min slot gets booked; a 60-min slot gets deferred. The written report carries the depth — the call carries the decision. If they want more time, they'll ask for a follow-up — which is a buying signal.

### The pilot transition (last 3 min)
> "Based on what we've found here, the architectural recommendation — sovereign deployment for privileged-matter workflows — is the only option that structurally addresses all three of the disclosure scenarios in Section 6.
>
> If you want to evaluate that path further, we offer a 90-day pilot of AION OS specifically designed for firms in your size range. The audit fee you've already paid credits 100% against the pilot deposit, which is [pricing — see pilot tier].
>
> No pressure to decide now. I'd suggest letting the report sit with your team for a week, then circling back if it's a direction you want to explore."

### Why this works
- Soft, not aggressive
- Honors the audit as a standalone deliverable
- Frames the pilot as a *natural next step*, not a sale
- Removes financial friction by crediting the audit fee

## 8. Objection Handling

### "We already did an internal AI audit last year."
> "Likely focused on use-case approval and data classification — what tools attorneys can use for what. This audit is the layer above that — what happens to those tools if the vendors themselves restructure. Different question, different deliverable."

### "Our IT team handles vendor risk."
> "IT handles operational vendor risk — uptime, security, breach response. This audit handles *contractual and architectural* vendor risk — what the vendors are allowed to change, and what disclosure obligations follow. It's a complement to IT's work, not a replacement."

### "$750/$1,500 is a lot for a report."
> "It's about 4-6 hours of a partner's time. The cost of *not* knowing what's in those contracts is the disclosure exposure in Section 6 of the report. Most firms find at least one clause in their current AI contracts they would have negotiated differently if they'd known."

### "How is this different from what [Big 4 / consulting firm] would do?"
> "Three differences. First, my work is built specifically for AI vendors, not generic SaaS — the failure modes are different. Second, the audit credits against a sovereign deployment if you decide to move that direction, so the cost is recoverable. Third, I'm a founder, not a partner-track consultant — turnaround is 2-3 weeks, not 2-3 months."

### "We don't use that much AI yet."
> "That's actually the best time to do this. The audit gives you a procurement framework so the next AI tool you bring in is contracted properly from day one. Easier to build the framework before you have legacy contracts to renegotiate."

### "Send me a sample report."
> "I can send a redacted excerpt — full reports are confidential to the engaging firm. Want me to send the table of contents and a sample finding from a prior engagement?"
>
> *(For your first audit you don't have a sample yet. Acceptable response: "This is a new offering — first three engagements are pricing-discounted in exchange for the option to use anonymized findings as future samples. Want to be one of the first three?")*

### "We need to think about it."
> "Absolutely. To make it easier — would it help if I sent the engagement letter and one-page scope so you have it in front of you when you discuss internally? No commitment until you sign."
