## FINANCIAL IMPACT DATA

### Departing Firm Losses

**Revenue Impact:**
- Average partner generates: **$1M-$5M annually** (AmLaw 200 data)
- IP/Corporate partners: **$3M-$8M** (higher specialization premium)
- Client defection rate: **40-60%** of partner's book follows within 18 months
- Institutional client retention drops: **25-35%** if partner held relationship

**Litigation Costs:**
- Restrictive covenant enforcement: **$250k-$1.5M** in legal fees
- Trade secret misappropriation claims: **$500k-$3M** (if goes to trial)
- Client poaching lawsuits: **$150k-$800k** (typically settle)
- Emergency TRO/injunction filings: **$75k-$150k** (first 30 days)

**Hidden Costs:**
- Morale impact on remaining attorneys: 15-20% productivity loss (first quarter)
- Recruitment costs to replace: $200k-$500k (headhunter fees, signing bonuses)
- Knowledge transfer failure: 30-40% of institutional knowledge lost permanently
- Client panic management: 50-100 billable hours redirected to retention calls

**Data Exfiltration Risks:**
- 67% of departing employees take confidential data (Ponemon Institute study)
- Legal sector: 78% admission rate in depositions (higher than general workforce)
- Most common targets:
  - Client lists and contact info
  - Billing rates and fee structures
  - Work product (briefs, memos, research)
  - Opposing counsel strategies
  - Pitch decks and business development materials

## TWO-SIDED RISK ANALYSIS

### Side 1: Departing Firm (Defense Mode)

**Critical 30-Day Window:**
- Day 1-7: Attorney has full access, begins "cleanup" (data exfiltration)
- Day 8-14: Notice given, firm scrambles to implement access controls
- Day 15-21: Transition period, attorney still touching client matters
- Day 22-30: Final days, highest risk for bulk downloads/prints

**What Firms Miss:**
1. **Email forwarding rules** - set to auto-forward to personal Gmail
2. **Cloud storage syncs** - Dropbox/OneDrive secretly syncing firm files
3. **Phone photos** - attorney photographing physical files in office
4. **Printer logs** - bulk printing of client lists at 6am before anyone arrives
5. **VPN access from home** - downloading case files to personal devices
6. **"Accidentally" CCing personal email** on client communications

**AION Value Prop:**
> "We identify the 6-8 attack vectors you're not monitoring. Most firms check email, but miss cloud storage rules, VPN logs, and physical document access. One bulk download at 2am costs you $2M in client revenue."

### Side 2: Hiring Firm (Offense/Due Diligence Mode)

**Lateral Hire Failure Rates:**
- 30% fail within 18 months (don't hit revenue targets)
- 45% cultural integration issues (create internal conflict)
- 20% bring ethical/legal liability (tainted data, conflicts of interest)

**What Hiring Firms Should Ask:**
1. **How clean was the departure?**
   - Did they give proper notice?
   - Were restrictive covenants properly addressed?
   - Any litigation threatened or filed?

2. **What data are they bringing?**
   - "Work product" vs. "trade secrets" (legal distinction matters)
   - Client contact info (ethical gray area)
   - Opposing counsel strategies (potential malpractice if used)

3. **What liabilities follow them?**
   - Pending bar complaints
   - Client grievances from old firm
   - Unpaid malpractice tail coverage
   - Conflicts of interest from old firm's client list

**AION Value Prop:**
> "Before you commit $500k in signing bonus + salary, let us run a 5-stage adversarial analysis. We'll tell you if they're bringing gold or a grenade. 20% of lateral hires we analyze have red flags the hiring firm missed."
