## Three Core Modules

### 1. Capacity Gap Analysis
**Purpose:** Identify the gap between where you are and where you want to be, then challenge every excuse keeping you there.

**What it does:**
- Reality check (strip away your narrative)
- Blind spot exposure (5 patterns most people miss)
- Hard questions (designed to make you uncomfortable)
- Growth prescription (do this or stay where you are)
- Accountability metrics (no hiding, no excuses)

**Use cases:**
- Career transitions (stuck in job vs wanting to start business)
- Personal development (where you are vs who you want to become)
- Relationship goals (current dynamics vs desired connection)
- Health/fitness (actual state vs target state)
- Financial goals (income now vs income needed)

**Example command:**
```bash
python -m aionos.api.capacity_cli analyze-gap \
  --current "Mid-level manager, comfortable salary, coasting" \
  --desired "VP-level leader driving strategy and building teams" \
  --intensity challenging \
  --fears "Failure, visibility, responsibility" \
  --obstacles "Lack of executive presence, no mentor, risk-averse"
```

**Output includes:**
- Gap severity assessment
- Hidden costs of staying where you are
- 5 blind spots blocking growth
- 10 hard questions you can't avoid
- 24-hour/7-day/30-day/90-day growth plan
- Accountability system with red flags

---

### 2. Emotional Intelligence Challenge
**Purpose:** Analyze your emotional patterns and get adversarial feedback on your EQ.

**What it does:**
- Identify emotional patterns (defensiveness, people-pleasing, avoidance, etc.)
- Expose blind spots in your responses
- Find your emotional growth edge
- Generate higher-EQ alternative responses
- Prescribe daily EQ-building exercises

**Use cases:**
- Processing difficult feedback
- Navigating conflict situations
- Understanding reactive patterns
- Building emotional maturity
- Improving relationship dynamics

**Example command:**
```bash
python -m aionos.api.capacity_cli eq-challenge \
  --situation "Boss gave me critical feedback in front of the team" \
  --response "I defended myself and explained why they were wrong" \
  --intensity confrontational
```

**Output includes:**
- Your emotional pattern (e.g., "Defensive Reactivity")
- What you missed in your response
- Your emotional growth edge
- What a higher-EQ version of you would say
- 5 daily practices to build emotional capacity

---

### 3. Belief Challenge
**Purpose:** Challenge your beliefs adversarially. Most beliefs are inherited, not examined.

**What it does:**
- Audit your beliefs (are they serving or sabotaging you?)
- Cui bono analysis (who benefits from you believing this?)
- Cost calculation (what is this belief costing you?)
- Generate alternative beliefs (what if the opposite were true?)
- Design experiments (test the belief, get data, update)

**Use cases:**
- Examining limiting beliefs ("I'm not a leader type")
- Questioning inherited narratives ("You need a degree to succeed")
- Testing assumptions ("Starting a business is too risky")
- Challenging identity stories ("I'm not good with people")
- Upgrading belief systems (replace scarcity with abundance mindset)

**Example command:**
```bash
python -m aionos.api.capacity_cli challenge-belief \
  --belief "I'm too old to make a career change" \
  --evidence-for "I'm 45, most successful people started younger" \
  --evidence-against "Colonel Sanders started KFC at 62, many pivot later" \
  --intensity adversarial
```

**Output includes:**
- Belief origin audit (where did this come from?)
- Who benefits analysis (cui bono?)
- Total cost of belief (opportunity, time, identity, financial)
- Alternative beliefs to test
- 7-day belief experiment design
