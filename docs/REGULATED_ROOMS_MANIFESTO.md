## II. Law Firms

### Where it's banned or formally restricted
- **Most AmLaw 100 firms** issued internal bans 2023-2024 after privileged work product was accidentally trained into models. Public examples: Mishcon de Reya, Allen & Overy (pre-merger), Linklaters, Hill Dickinson.
- **Any matter under a litigation hold** — sending privileged documents to a third-party AI = potential waiver of attorney-client privilege.
- **Government legal departments** — DOJ attorneys, state AGs, federal public defenders all operate under generative AI restrictions.

### The legal basis
- **ABA Formal Opinion 512 (July 2024)** — direct guidance to attorneys on generative AI risks.
- **ABA Model Rule 1.6 (Confidentiality)** — most state bars interpret this to prohibit cloud-AI use without explicit client consent.
- **State bar opinions** — Florida, California, New York have all issued formal ethics warnings.

### The pitch
> *"Privileged data sent to OpenAI is privileged data sent to a third party. Most bar associations call that a waiver. AION never sends it anywhere."*

## III. Healthcare

### Where it's banned
- **Hospital EHR systems** (Epic, Cerner, Allscripts) — cloud AI use = HIPAA violation.
- **Major academic medical centers** banned ChatGPT on clinical workstations 2023-2024: Mass General, Mayo Clinic, Stanford Health, Johns Hopkins.
- **Clinical trial environments** under FDA 21 CFR Part 11 — AI tools without validated audit trails are non-compliant.
- **VA hospitals** — Department of Veterans Affairs prohibits public AI on systems with PHI.

### The legal basis
- **HIPAA** — requires Business Associate Agreements with anyone touching PHI. OpenAI offers BAAs only on enterprise tier; many hospital legal teams won't sign even then.
- **FDA 21 CFR Part 11** — electronic records compliance for clinical trials.
- **HHS/OCR enforcement** — fines $100-$50K per record.

### The pitch
> *"HIPAA fines start at $100 per record. A single accidental ChatGPT paste with 1,000 patient records = $100,000 minimum. AION doesn't put you in that room."*

## IV. Defense / National Security

### Where it's banned (literally illegal to use)
- **SCIFs** (Sensitive Compartmented Information Facilities) — air-gapped by design under **ICD 705**. ChatGPT physically cannot reach these rooms.
- **SAP environments** (Special Access Programs) — even more restrictive than SCIFs.
- **Classified networks** — SIPRNet (Secret), JWICS (Top Secret) — air-gapped from the public internet.
- **CMMC-certified contractor environments** — Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC 2.0 effective late 2025) makes most public AI use non-compliant.
- **ITAR-controlled work** — International Traffic in Arms Regulations. Sending ITAR data to OpenAI = federal crime. Up to 20 years and $1M per violation.
- **DoD-wide** — formal guidance restricting generative AI on DoD systems issued 2023, expanded 2024.

### The legal basis
- **Espionage Act, Arms Export Control Act** — criminal statutes.
- **ITAR (22 CFR 120-130)** — controls on technical data sharing with foreign persons.
- **Executive Order 14110** — federal AI risk management framework.

### The pitch
> *"In a SCIF, ChatGPT isn't 'discouraged.' It's physically impossible. The room is air-gapped by federal regulation. AION is the only architecture allowed in those rooms."*
