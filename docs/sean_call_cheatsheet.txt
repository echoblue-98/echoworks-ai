FLOW: 6 MOVES (CONVERSATION, NOT A PITCH)
------------------------------------------

1. OPEN — GET TO KNOW HIM (3-5 min)
   "Dan connected us — appreciate you making time."
   "How long have you been in the litigation space?"
   "What drew you to Hathaway & Coons?"
   "What does your practice look like day to day?"

   → Just listen. Build rapport. Learn how he talks about
     his work. His language becomes your language later.

2. PROBE THE PAIN (5 min)
   Let him surface it. Don't lead him there.

   "Have you ever lost an attorney to a competitor?"
   "What happened to the client relationships?"
   "In commercial lit — when someone leaves, how do you
    know what they accessed in their last few weeks?"
   "On the energy side — those lease docs, mineral rights —
    who has access and who's watching?"
   "Has anything ever walked out the door that you found
    out about too late?"

   → Whatever he says, go deeper: "How did that play out?"
     The pain is in the details he tells you.

3. CONTEXTUALIZE FOR A REGIONAL FIRM (3 min)
   Ground it in his world, not Big Law stats.

   "At the national level, firms average 77 days to detect
    insider theft. For a regional firm without a dedicated
    security team, it's usually longer — because nobody's
    watching."

   "Most firms your size either don't have DLP or have it
    turned off because 90% of the alerts are noise."

   Don't quote $8M. Instead:
   "What's your biggest client relationship worth annually?"
   Whatever he says → "That's what's at stake if someone
   stages files for two weeks and you don't see it."

4. HANDLE HIDDEN OBJECTIONS WITH CONVICTION (2-3 min)
   If "we're too small" or "never had a problem" surfaces:

   "AI isn't coming — it's already here. 365, all the way
    live. The question is whether it's working for your
    firm or you're just hoping nothing happens."

   "The firms that wait find out 77 days later. The ones
    that act find out in 3 seconds. The difference isn't
    budget — it's awareness."

   "One departure crisis — the legal fees, the client
    recovery, the reputational hit — costs more than
    years of prevention. This saves that."

5. SEGUE INTO THEIR CURRENT SECURITY (3 min)
   This is the bridge to the close.

   "What does Hathaway & Coons use right now for security
    and compliance around client confidentiality?"

   "Who manages that — internal IT, an MSP, or is it
    more ad hoc?"

   "How do you handle data governance for things like
    attorney-client privilege and work product?"

   → Whatever the answer, the gap reveals itself.
     If they have tools → "Those tools watch files.
     We watch behavior. Different layer entirely."
     If they have nothing → "That's actually common.
     And it's exactly why we built this to deploy in
     48 hours with zero disruption."

   THE MOAT (say this clearly):
   "Everything runs 100% on your hardware. Zero data
    egress. No client files, no PII, no matter data
    ever leaves your network. That's not a feature —
    that's the architecture."

   "$5,000 a month. We deploy in 48 hours, read-only.
    We baseline every attorney's access patterns. If we

   If yes → "I'll send the retainer agreement tonight."
   If needs time → "When's good to follow up this week?"
